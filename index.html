Point the top edge of your device toward the sea and slowly turn. The results update live as your heading changes — the next three countries, the seas between them, and the nearest city to each landfall.

How this works & limitations
  • Uses your device's compass (magnetometer) and GPS location — everything runs in your browser, no server involved.
  • Works best on an iPhone or iPad in Safari. Most desktop browsers and many Android browsers don't expose a compass heading.
  • The heading is relative to magnetic north, not true north, and can be thrown off by nearby metal or magnets.
  • It follows a great-circle path (the true "straight line" on a sphere) up to 20,000 km, listing the next three countries you'd cross, the seas between them, and the nearest city to each landfall.
  • Countries are sovereign states from Natural Earth, so the UK shows as "United Kingdom" rather than "England", etc.
  • Landmass detection uses a simplified world coastline (Natural Earth 110m). Small islands or narrow spits of land may be missed.

Data: Natural Earth · No backend, hosted on GitHub Pages.
